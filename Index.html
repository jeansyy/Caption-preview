A Better You – Healthy Ways to Avoid Microplastics

Microplastics are tiny plastic bits in your water, food, and even your bloodstream.

1. Choose Tap Water Over Bottled

Filtered tap water usually contains fewer microplastics than bottled. Skip the plastic when you can.

2. Don’t Heat Food in Plastic

Heating plastic containers releases microplastics into your food. Use glass or ceramic instead.

3. Cut Down on Processed Foods

Heavily processed foods can contain higher levels of plastic. Cooking whole foods helps reduce exposure.

4. Watch Packaging

Plastics and painted bottle caps can shed particles. Go for glass jars, paper packaging, and fresh produce.

5. Wear Natural Fibers

Polyester and nylon shed microfibers when washed. Switch to cotton, hemp, or wool when you can.

Small changes = big impact. A better you starts with fewer plastics.
